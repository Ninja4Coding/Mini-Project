Nobel Peace Prize 2025

The Nobel Peace Prize for 2025 was awarded for significant contributions to humanitarian efforts and global peace initiatives, recognizing the role of civil society.

Key Highlights :

  • Awarded for contributions that foster dialogue and social cohesion, the prize emphasized the importance of civic engagement in achieving lasting peace.
  • The Nobel Committee emphasized the recipients' efforts in humanitarian assistance and their commitment to protecting human rights in conflict zones.
  • The prize was jointly awarded to three organizations recognized for their roles in promoting diplomacy and conflict resolution across various regions.

India's New Ports as Green Hydrogen Hubs

Green hydrogen is produced through the electrolysis of water using renewable energy sources, such as wind, solar, or hydroelectric power, resulting in zero carbon emissions.

Key Highlights :

  • The Indian government recognized three ports as green hydrogen hubs to promote sustainable energy solutions and enhance energy security.
  • India's move to designate ports as green hydrogen hubs is part of a larger strategy to transition to renewable energy and reduce carbon emissions.
  • Many countries are engaging in international partnerships to share knowledge, research, and technology to advance green hydrogen projects globally.

UK PM Keir Starmer's Visit

From October 8 to 9, 2025, UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer visited India. Key highlights included a focus on trade relations, the launch of the India-UK Fintech partnership, and plans for UK university campuses in India.

Key Highlights :

  • The visit aimed at enhancing India-UK trade relations, with significant investment announcements and collaborations, particularly in the fintech sector.
  • CEOs from both countries participated in discussions to solidify economic and academic partnerships.
